# Brand Brief — TennisBootcamp.ca

## Tone
Welcoming, serious, athletic, premium. The voice should feel like a world-class coach speaking directly to a motivated player — not a salesperson and not a tech company.

## What to Avoid
- SaaS or tech-network visual language (gradients, floating UI cards, glassmorphism for its own sake)
- Generic startup design patterns
- Visual clutter or competing calls-to-action
- Extra CTAs that dilute the primary conversion goal
- Anything that looks like it belongs on a productivity app

## Hero Section
The hero should feel dynamic and high-performance. If animation is used, it must feel tennis-related — ball physics, racket motion, court geometry — never abstract tech motion. The hero's job is to communicate elite training at first glance and drive the primary CTA.

## CTA Hierarchy
1. **Get Priority Placement** (primary — links to `/intake`)
2. **View Programs** (secondary — links to program details)
3. **Newsletter signup** (tertiary — low-friction, no competition with primary)

## Visual Direction
- Athletic and clean: generous whitespace, strong typography, high-contrast accents
- Court colors and textures are appropriate when used with restraint
- Photography or motion should feature real tennis action, not staged lifestyle shots
- No stock-photo feel
